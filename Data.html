This link provides an R-file that contains all the data sets. You should run this R file once, and all data will be available in the R system. The data sets are also included in the R package.


The separate data sets can also be downloaded from the links below. They are in .csv format.

  1. dice.csv (Weldon’s dice data)

  2. lifetimes.csv (lifetimes data)

  3. polonium.csv (polonium data)

  4. prusian.csv (Prusian army data)

  5. river.csv (river data)

  6. tensile.csv (tensile strength data)

  7. PCB.csv (chemical concentration data)

  8. birth.csv (birth-time data)

  9. artificial1.csv (artificial series 1 data)

  10. artificial2.csv (artificial series 2 data)

  11. artificial3.csv (artificial series 3 data)

  12. industrial4.csv(industrial series 4 data)

  13. industrial5.csv(industrial series 5 data)

  14. industrial6.csv(industrial series 6 data)

  15. cells.csv (bacterial cells data)

  16. stems.csv (black bean aphid data)

  17. lamb.csv (foetal lamb movements)

  18. FairIsle.csv (annual maximum December temperatures at Fair Isle)

  19. aluminium.csv (aluminium defects data)

  20. lamp.csv (life testing of incandescent lamps)

  21. flood.csv (flood heights)

  22. sea.csv (Port Pirie annual maximum December sea levels)
